Don't let dreams be dreams

A brief review of 2016 of Leo Yeung

About Me

Hi! I am Leo, from Hong Kong.

2016 has been a fruitful year.
I have done tons of stuff that I have longed to do and discovered a lot more I wanted to pursuit.
Having plateaued in my previous job, I decided to step out of my comfort zone. And it was the best decision in my life.

I travelled to places that I did not even dare to dream of before -- the profile pic and the landing page photos were taken in Salar de Uyuni, in Bolivia, by me and my friends whom I met when travelling in Peru.

Making friends from different cultures, trying out different lifestyles and daring myself to conquer new challenges are surely unforgettable experience that I would treasure for the rest of my life.

Eventually, I travelled for six months in Europe and South America and ended up learning coding for four months in Silicon Valley.(Wait! What?)

One may wonder, why would it end up this way?
Nobody, including me, would have thought that would happen. And this brings us to my application of the summer cohort of Founders and Coders 2017.

The Trip

I started in Germany, then travelled to in Austria, Czech, Hungary, Greece, Denmark, Sweden, United Kingdom (I figured I could use more than four days), Spain, then to Peru, Bolivia; and at that time decided to pursuit a free coding school in Silicon Valley so I cut my trip short and returned to Hong Kong via Chile and Australia to make my US visa. And eventually spent four months in US.

So technically speaking I have travelled five continents except Africa and Antarctica. And took more flights than the rest of my life combined.
Create a lot of new personal records as well. First time in Europe, First time in America, first time using Couchsurfing (my first host taught me how to dumpster-dive), etc. and found out a lot about myself, like afraid of height(after biking down a 4000 meters high mountain).

42 in Silicon Valley

The free coding school in Silicon Valley is 42 USA.
42 is a revolutionary school which offers free coding education, through peer to peer project based learning.
No teachers, no classes and no schedules, students, from all sorts of background, ranging from newbie to experienced developer, learn at their own pace and through their peers.
Before becoming a student, one must go through online exams and a gruelling, one-month-long coding exam -- The Piscine.

The Piscine, which means "Swimming Pool" in French, was a one-month-long exam in C and candidates were allowed to use only certain functions from the unix standard library to complete daily tasks.
That was the hardest and the most gruelling exam that I have ever taken as I had no programming background by then. I worked more than 90 hours a week to catch up in the exam and eventually got in as one of the top 10% of the batch. Around one third of the batch made it.
That was one of the most intense learning experience and the steepest learning curve I have ever had.

After entering the program, apart from coding the projects in the curriculum, which are mainly in C, I attended all kinds of talks, conference and joined hackathons; these really changed my perspectives about Tech, as the environment and atmosphere are completely different than those in Hong Kong.
The photo on the right is about my friends in 42 and I winning a hackathon. We built a smart home app using smell for event notification, using javascript to program the AI in Amazon Alexa and Google home.
And I am very satisfied about my progress, as I started from zero several months ago.

I loved the experience and embraced those changes. But unfortunately, due to VISA issues, I had to go back to Hong Kong and suspended my study.

But I still want to learn more in coding, especially in web development and javascript, then I found out about Founders and Coders...

My next stop is ...

Founders and Coders, I hope.
And I really buy the idea of peer to peer project based learning as I tried and loved it when I was in 42. I believe it is the future.
I love intense and immersive learning methodology as well. And FAC has a good track record of getting its graduates in Tech, the curriculum is also focusing on the latest web technologies, which I need to brush up on.
Looking forward to join FAC and make a bang in Tech in London.
